Methodology

The Wonder Mum Foundation will employ a holistic and community-driven methodology to achieve its objectives. This includes:

  • Needs Assessment Conduct surveys and interviews to understand the specific challenges faced by the target demographic. Analyze existing data on teen pregnancies, HIV infection, and drug resistance in the community.
  • Collaborative Partnerships Forge partnerships with schools, healthca reproviders, and community organizations to amplify the impact of interventions. Engage with local leaders and influencers to promote community involvement and acceptance.
  • Tailored Educational Programs- Develop and implement age-appropriate, culturally sensitive, and evidence-based sex education programs. - Provide workshops on HIV prevention, testing, and treatment options. - Disseminate information on responsible antibiotic use through community workshops and informational campaigns.

Findings

Based on preliminary research and community engagement, initial findings highlight a significant lack of accessible information on sexual health, contributing to the challenges of teen pregnancies, HIV infection, and drug resistance. Stigma and cultural barriers also emerged as impediments to seeking support and resources. These findings underscore the critical need for the Wonder Mum Foundation's intervention to bridge these gaps and empower adolescents in making informed choices for a healthier future.

Overview

The project aims to address critical emerging issues surrounding Teens, driven by natural curiosity and the desire for intimacy, may engage in sexual activity. Unfortunately, some find themselves facing the unintended consequence of teen pregnancies. As they grapple with the challenges of early parenthood, an added layer of complexity emerges when they discover HIV infection. The subsequent journey involves navigating the complexities of HIV treatment, introducing antiretroviral (ARV) drugs into their lives. Over time, the difficulties they endure, including societal stigmas and economic pressures, may contribute to the emergence of ARV drug resistance, further complicating their health and well-being. Recognizing the interconnected nature of these challenges, our initiative seeks to empower adolescents with knowledge, skills, and resources to make informed decisions about their sexual health, prevent the spread of HIV, and combat drug resistance. By fostering collaboration between healthcare professionals, educators, and community leaders, we aim to create a holistic support system that enables young individuals to navigate these complex issues and lead healthier lives.
