About Us

We Help People In Need Around The World

The genesis of the Wonder Mum Foundation finds its roots in a deeply personal journey inspired by the unwavering dedication of my birth mother, a compassionate psychologist and counselor specializing in HIV-infected adolescents. Her tireless efforts in providing psychological support and guidance to young individuals navigating the challenges of HIV have been both inspiring and transformative. Through her work, my awareness of the intersecting issues of teen pregnancies, HIV infection, and drug resistance became profoundly heightened. Witnessing the struggles and triumphs of adolescents grappling with these complex issues, I was inspired to establish the Wonder Mum Foundation. The name itself pays homage to the resilience and nurturing qualities emblematic of motherhood, encapsulating the spirit of support, care, and empowerment that lies at the heart of our initiative. Informed by the invaluable insights gained from my mother's experiences, the Wonder Mum Foundation seeks to create a compassionate and informed space for adolescents, ensuring they have access to the resources, knowledge, and support necessary to make informed choices regarding their sexual health. The commitment to addressing these challenges is not just a mission; it is a personal journey fueled by the desire to make a meaningful difference in the lives of young individuals, building a foundation for a healthier and more empowered future.

Jane Olali, Team member

Our mission is to implement a multifaceted initiative that educates, supports, and empowers adolescents in addressing the challenges of teen pregnancies, HIV infection, and drug resistance.

Activities

Education & Awareness

    KEY ACTIVITIES
  • Conduct workshops and seminars on HIV prevention, testing, and treatment options.
  • Create and distribute informational materials addressing responsible antibiotic use
  • Launch targeted campaigns to raise awareness about the interconnected issues
INTENDED OUTCOME
  • Increased awareness and adoption of preventive measures & safe practices e.g. condom use, and pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP).
  • Increased testing rates among the teens
  • Reduction of HIV-related stigma

Community Engagement

    KEY ACTIVITIES
  • Establish community partnerships with schools, healthcare providers, and local organizations.
  • Organize community forums and discussions to address stigma surrounding teen pregnancies and HIV.
  • Engage local leaders and influencers to promote community involvement and acceptance.
INTENDED OUTCOME
  • Reducing the incidence of unplanned pregnancies and the spread of HIV.
  • Collective responsibility towards the well-being of adolescents.
  • Positive shifts in behavior at both individual and community levels,

Advocacy and Policy Influence

    KEY ACTIVITIES
  • Advocate for comprehensive sex education policies in schools.
  • Lobby for policies promoting access to sexual health services for adolescents.
  • Participate in campaigns advocating responsible antibiotic use at the community and policy levels.
INTENDED OUTCOME
  • Available funding allocation towards programs and initiatives targeting teen pregnancies, contributing to scalability and sustainability of effective interventions.

Resource Development

    KEY ACTIVITIES
  • Develop a centralized online resource hub for adolescents providing information on sexual health and HIV.
  • Create and distribute informational brochures, pamphlets, and posters in schools and community centers.
INTENDED OUTCOME
  • Interactive Learning Modules
  • Q&A and Expert Forums
  • Real Stories and Testimonials
  • Virtual Reality (VR) Experiences
  • Multilingual Accessibility
  • Live Webinars and Workshops
